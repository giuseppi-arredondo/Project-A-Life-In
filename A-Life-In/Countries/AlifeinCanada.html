Canada, located in North America, is the second largest country in the world by land area, with a population of approximately 38 million people. Ottawa is the capital city, while Toronto is the largest city. English and French are the official languages, and the currency is the Canadian dollar (CAD). Canada operates as a federal parliamentary democracy and constitutional monarchy.

People are attracted to living in Canada for its high quality of life, beautiful natural landscapes, diverse cities, and welcoming immigration policies. Notable personalities from Canada include Justin Trudeau (Prime Minister), Celine Dion (singer), and Ryan Reynolds (actor).

Canada's minimum wage varies by province but averages around 15 CAD per hour, translating to approximately 2,400 CAD per month or roughly 1,850 USD depending on the exchange rate. The healthcare system, known as Medicare, provides publicly funded essential medical services to all citizens and permanent residents. Education is also publicly funded and accessible up to secondary school, with high academic standards.

Politically, Canada is known for its stability, inclusiveness, and commitment to multiculturalism. The country is divided into ten provinces and three territories, each with its own government under a federal system that values democracy and equality.

The cost of living varies across regions, with Toronto and Vancouver being the most expensive. Despite high housing costs, salaries and public benefits generally balance living standards.
