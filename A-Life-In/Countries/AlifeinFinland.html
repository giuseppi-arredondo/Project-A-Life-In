A Life in Finland (FIN)

Finland is a Northern European country admired for its clean nature, high education, and advanced technology sector. Below is essential information for anyone considering life in Finland.

Finland, located in Northern Europe, is known for its stunning natural landscapes, advanced technology sector, and high quality of life. Helsinki is the capital and largest city, with a population of approximately 5.5 million. Finnish and Swedish are the official languages, and the currency is the Euro (EUR).

Finland offers top-tier healthcare and education systems, generous welfare benefits, and an emphasis on equality and safety. The average monthly salary ranges between 3,500 and 4,000 EUR, with collective agreements determining wages.

Healthcare is publicly funded, providing universal coverage. Education is free and compulsory from ages 7 to 16, with higher education largely subsidized.

Politically, Finland is a stable parliamentary republic known for transparency and innovation. The country values sustainability, technological development, and social inclusion.

Emigrating to Finland is accessible through work, study, or family visas. The country seeks skilled professionals and innovators, making it a top destination for those looking to thrive in an advanced and peaceful society.
